My Research

General Interest


My research interest mainly focuses on the theoretical analysis of different institutions, like different forms of conflict, cooperation, design of mechanism, etc.. I investigate the interactive relationship between individuals, institutions, and individuals and institutions. Both behavioral and welfare insights are studied, based on which I make policy implications. The major tools I employ are from game theory.


Work in Progress


Title: Does Ex-Ante Asymmetry Matter? A Modeling of Multi-Player Asymmetric War of Attrition.

Abstract:
This paper models a multi-player asymmetric war of attrition game with incomplete information on private provision of public goods to investigate how ex-ante asymmetry affects behavior and welfare. In the unique equilibrium, asymmetry differentiates types into distinct incentive positions which lead to a stratified behavior pattern that one player exits instantly with positive probability, while each of others has no probability of concession until a certain moment associated with them. Efficiency measured by the cost of delay is mainly determined by the strongest type, defined as the highest type of the instant-exit player, which influences the ranking of the incentive positions of all types and therefore leads their behavior. Besides, if the asymmetry is introduced by strengthening the strongest type, it always improves efficiency, whereas if the strongest type is controlled, the effect of asymmetry coincides with the sign of an intuitive measure of the cost of symmetry.
